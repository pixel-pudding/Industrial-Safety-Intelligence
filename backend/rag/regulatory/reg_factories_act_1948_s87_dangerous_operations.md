## Verbatim excerpt

> "Where the State Government is of opinion that any manufacturing process
> or operation carried on in a factory exposes any persons employed in it
> to a serious risk of bodily injury, poisoning or disease, it may make
> rules applicable to any factory or class or description of factories in
> which the manufacturing process or operation is carried on..."

Under this power the State Government may (per Section 87, summarized from
the six enumerated clauses): declare specific processes dangerous; restrict
employment of women, adolescents, or children in them; require periodic
medical examination and fitness certification of workers; require
protective measures for all persons in the vicinity of the dangerous
operation (not only those directly engaged in it); prohibit, restrict, or
control the use of specified hazardous materials or processes; and require
welfare, sanitary, and protective-equipment standards specific to the
operation.

## Relevance to SPSCL platform

Section 87's "protection for all persons in the vicinity" clause is the
statutory basis for treating worker presence as a genuine risk factor
across zone boundaries, not only inside the zone officially performing the
hazardous operation — e.g. Zone D hot work with elevated VOC-CONC drifting
in from the adjacent Zone C tank farm boundary is a Section 87 vicinity
concern for Zone C personnel too, not just Zone D permit holders. SPSCL's
EO/EG reactor process (Zone A) and chlorine electrolysis (Zone B) are the
kind of processes this section anticipates being declared "dangerous" or
"hazardous" under the First Schedule framework, which is also the basis
for the Factories Act's broader hazardous-process disclosure and
on-site-emergency-plan obligations that the Quality & Compliance Audit
Agent checks against.
